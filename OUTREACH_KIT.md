### Q1: "How can I increase my typing speed for SSC CHSL?"
> The SSC CHSL typing test needs 35 WPM (English) or 30 WPM (Hindi) over 10
> minutes, and most people fail not from slow fingers but from three habits:
> looking at the keyboard, chasing speed before accuracy, and practicing only
> 1-minute tests then collapsing in a 10-minute exam.
>
> What worked for candidates I've seen:
> 1. **Accuracy first** — practice at 95%+ before pushing speed. Speed built on
>    errors falls apart under exam pressure.
> 2. **Practice the exact 10-minute format daily** — stamina is a skill too.
> 3. **Fix your 3 slowest keys** after each test instead of re-typing the whole
>    thing.
>
> Give yourself 4–6 weeks of daily 20-minute practice and 35 WPM is very
> achievable. I use this free SSC CHSL practice test (10-min format, English +
> Hindi, live WPM/accuracy): https://fasttypinglab.com/ssc-chsl-typing-test/

### Q2: "Which is the best free Hindi typing test for CPCT / UP Police?"
> It depends on the layout your exam uses — and this trips a LOT of people up:
> - **CPCT** uses **Mangal (Unicode/INSCRIPT)**.
> - **UP Police / Bihar SSC** often use **Kruti Dev**.
>
> They have completely different key positions, so practicing the wrong one
> wastes weeks. Always check your notification first.
>
> For free practice in both, I use FastTypingLab — it has separate Mangal and
> Kruti Dev tests with an on-screen keyboard and live WPM/accuracy:
> - CPCT (Mangal): https://fasttypinglab.com/cpct-typing-test/
> - UP Police (Kruti Dev): https://fasttypinglab.com/up-police-typing-test/

### Q3: "How many WPM is a good typing speed?"
> Rough benchmarks: average adults type ~40 WPM, comfortable touch typists
> 50–65, professionals 70–90, and 100+ is top 1%. For Indian govt exams the bar
> is usually 30–40 WPM.
>
> But accuracy matters more than raw speed — 70 WPM at 85% accuracy is
> effectively slower than 55 WPM at 99%, because every mistake needs correcting.
> Chase accuracy first; speed follows.
>
> You can check your current WPM free here (1-minute test):
> https://fasttypinglab.com/tests/

### Q4: "How can I practice Kruti Dev typing online for free?"
> Kruti Dev is a font-based layout (not Unicode), so you need to learn its
> specific key positions — reading a chart isn't enough, you learn by typing
> with the layout visible.
>
> Focus early practice on matras and half-letters (क्ष, त्र, ज्ञ) — that's where
> most people lose speed. 15 minutes a day for a couple of weeks makes a big
> difference.
>
> Free Kruti Dev practice with an on-screen layout + live WPM:
> https://fasttypinglab.com/kruti-dev-typing/
